Strategic Cost and Management Accounting

What is Strategy?

Strategy is the way to achieve the long term goal of an organization. It is a high level plan for a long term objective. It is based on the entity’s objectives, analyzing the competitive environment, assessing internal capabilities and determining the best course of action to gain an advantage or solve a problem.

So, strategy determines basic long term goals and objectives of an entity, adopt a course of action and allocate required resources to achieve the set goals and objectives.

Strategy focuses primarily on determination of basic long-term goals and objectives, adoption of course of action and allocation of resources for carrying out the set goals and objectives.

Organizations formulate strategy to set directions to outperform competitors.

Mintzberg (1987) provides 5 (Five) dimensions of strategy:

  1. Plan
  2. Ploy
  3. Pattern
  4. Position
  5. Perspectives

Let’s discuss in details:

  1. Plan:

It refers to setting some sort of consciously intended course of action, guidelines to seal with situations. It has two characteristics-it is developed purposely and in advance.

Tools: It identifies opportunities through PEST Analysis, SWOT Analysis and reaches opportunities through change management techniques.

  1. Ploy:

Every business has competitors and avoidance of competition is not possible. So businesses employ specific tactics to try and outperform or disrupt competitors.

For example-

        > dropping prices for defer competitors

        > filing patents

        > threatening legal actions

Ploy tends to short term tactics and five importance not to competitors but to own business strategy.

Tools: Scenario planning, Game theory, Future wheel etc.

  1. Pattern:

A business entity or organization gets the idea of strategy  from its every day actions taken. It analyzes the historical pattern of the actions and performance and behavior of the actions and then selects the patterns that should continue or enhance. You can say plan is the intended strategy and pattern is the realized strategy.

Tools: Core Competencies Model.

  1. Position:

Where the organization sits on the playing field relative to competitors. How an organization positions itself in the competitive environment or in the marketplace.

Example- cheap product but no compromise in quality

Tools: PEST Analysis, Value Chain Analysis, Porter’s Diamond Model

  1. Perspective:

It is about internal organizational perspective or culture. Attitude of employees or shared minded innovative focused culture.

Example: Apple’s innovation-driven culture shapes all decisions.

Tools: Core Competencies Model, Trompenaars Cultural Dimensions

Mintzberg argued that understanding these five different perspectives of strategy provides a richer and more comprehensive view of what strategy truly is and how it is formed within organizations.
